Welcome to Mad Libs! Mad Libs is a phrasal template word game originally created by Leonard Stern and Roger Price in 1958. It is frequently played as a party game or during pastime.

To play the game, fill in the blanks on the index card. Up to nine characters can be added to each blank. When you are ready, click on the colorful blue-purple circle button at the bottom to reveal a story.

Let your imagination run wild! The more engaging your language, the more captivating your story will be.

If you are a little rusty with your grammar, here is a quick refresher:

Adjective: A word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun. Example: big, red, one, alive.

  • The big dog barked.
  • That paint is blue.

Noun: A word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Example: apple, chicken, George, Santa Barbara.

  • I'm meeting Henry at the library today.
  • Look at that cute dog!

Verb: A word that describes an action, occurrence, or state of being. Example: run, was, is, have.

  • I run to the coffee shop every morning.
  • I have finished my homework.
