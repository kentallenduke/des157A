Cheeks, my family's Ameraucana chicken, is a clingy and affectionate bird. She's been following us around and participating in our everyday activities since she hatched four months ago. When we sit outside, she jumps onto our laps and demands cuddles. Her favorite pastime is sunbathing and watching us read outside.

In April 2023, my family bought three baby chickens from an old poultry store in Moorpark, a small town in southern California where we're from. Since I was away at school in Davis, my sister took care of them and let them outside to enjoy the sun. They are incredibly cute and love to bask in the sunlight.

Ube, our Lavender Orpington chicken, is the epitome of laziness. She strolls around the lawn and requires a bit of coaxing with treats to come to us. Her nonchalant demeanor means she doesn't mind being picked up, and if you place her on your lap, her laziness takes over and she falls asleep. Her favorite pastimes are sleeping and staring at the trees.

My chickens adore spending time with us outside. They'll nibble on our shoes, fly into our laps, or beg for treats. In this photo, Cheeks is perched on my sister's lap, and Ube is plotting to take her place.
